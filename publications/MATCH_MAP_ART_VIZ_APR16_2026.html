CORNSTALK: EQUATION DERIVATION + WOLFRAM CONVERGENCE
The Universal Kernel
dX/dt = lambda(t) * X(t)
This equation governs exponential growth with time-varying rate. It appears in population biology, compound interest, radioactive decay, epidemic spread, and capability compounding. It is not a metaphor. It is the literal dynamical equation.
Derivation

Step 1: Continuous Solution

X(t) = X_0 * exp( integral_0^t lambda(s) ds )

The general solution to dX/dt = lambda(t)*X(t) with initial condition X(0) = X_0. When lambda is constant, this reduces to simple exponential growth X(t) = X_0 * e^(lambda*t).

Step 2: Discrete Compounding Form

X(t) = X_0 * product_i (1 + lambda_i)^(t_i)

In practice, growth compounds in discrete intervals (overnight sessions, weekly sprints, deployment cycles). Each interval i has its own rate lambda_i applied for duration t_i. This is the MASTER EQUATION form used throughout WayneIA.

Step 3: N-Stalk Multiplier

P(at least one hit) = 1 - product_i (1 - p_i) for n independent vectors effective_lambda = 1 - product_i (1 - lambda_i)

With n independent vectors (stalks), the probability of at least one success follows complementary product form -- identical to the routing thesis R = 1 - product(1 - p_n). Multiple stalks compound the effective growth rate.
