The single biggest jump — +19 points from one architectural change on the same model — was the move from one-shot batch evaluation to SC-TIR (Self-Correcting Tool-Integrated Reasoning): the model calls a Python interpreter during reasoning, code executes in persistent Jupyter, results feed back, the model corrects, and only then is the final answer voted on by an 8-worker entropy-weighted majority. Same GPT-OSS-120B underneath. Different computational interface. 2× score from a routing decision, not a model decision.

This is the same thesis that animates the whole wayneColt substrate — data and models are commodities; routing is intelligence — and AIMO-3 was the externally-scored validation of it under tournament conditions.

The Lottery Math

By April 7, the canonical configuration (v28) was producing scores distributed approximately N(39.7, 1.7) across runs. The configuration was right. The remaining variance was sigma-noise — irreducible randomness in 8-worker entropy-weighted voting against a 50-problem test set with stochastic sandbox timing.

At that point the operator decision is binary:

  1. Keep modifying the configuration chasing a higher mean. Empirically what happened: every modification (MetaAgent, T=0.5, structured prompts, clustering) made it worse. The signal was beneath the noise floor and our ablations were confounded.
  2. Accept the configuration is right and play the lottery. Push the same config repeatedly, let the variance work for you. With N(39.7, 1.7) and Bronze at 42, P(score ≥ 42) ≈ 8% per submission. Five lottery tickets ≈ 35% cumulative. Ten ≈ 56%.

v29's 41 was a +1.8σ draw — a good lottery ticket that landed just under the threshold. The honest read: with two more lottery submissions on April 13-14 (instead of trying yet another configuration modification that turned out to hurt), we had a real shot at the +2.0σ draw that would have cleared 42. That's the operator-discipline lesson.

The April 15 Failure Mode

v40 was queued in Kaggle on the deadline day. It sat in QUEUED status for six hours and never executed before the competition closed. Kaggle GPU contention is documented as extreme in the final 24 hours of any competition; we knew this; we submitted late anyway. Submit-early discipline is the deadline-day equivalent of not modifying the configuration once it's right.

What This Validates About the Substrate

  1. SC-TIR architecture works under tournament conditions. The same Arcuate-Fasciculus thesis (specialized routing layer between a base model and a tool surface) that animates the BABEL escalation pipeline produced a 2× score improvement on a math-reasoning benchmark. Routing-as-intelligence is empirically grounded, not aspirational.
  2. Operator discipline matters more than model choice once configuration is right. The 11 modification attempts after v28 all regressed because the sigma-noise dominated the signal we were chasing. Recognizing the shift from optimization-mode to lottery-mode is the operator skill that separates 41 from 30 and possibly from 43.
  3. $95 / 0.18-rank competence floor. Top 18.3% on a 4,138-team competition for under $100 is a benchmark on what an I-35-resident operator with a sovereign-local substrate can produce incidentally, not as a primary product.
